- Target Market: NA-English
- Genre: Queer Campus Scandal Romance / Enemies to Lovers / Fake Dating
- Episode Count: 24

- Core Mood / Visual Tags: bruise-blue winter, cold prestige, public scandal, church power, dangerous tenderness
- Protagonist Snapshot: Ezra turns truth into a weapon; Cade turns survival into a choice.

## Project Positioning And World Background

The series lives inside a premium New England Catholic hockey campus where image is currency. St. Brendan College sells discipline, tradition, donor loyalty, and male excellence. Under that polished surface is an old machine that protects winning boys, wealthy fathers, and institutional silence.

This is not a soft campus romance. The world is built on collision:

- public holiness versus private violence
- athlete branding versus actual damage
- rumor versus evidence
- fake intimacy versus real trust

The emotional core remains intimate and queer, but the dramatic container is public. Hearings, donor events, podcast recordings, locker-room politics, and church optics turn private fear into watchable external stakes.

## Timeline And Backstory

- Three years ago: Noah Quinn, then a rising prep-school player, is assaulted after a locker-room purity crackdown. The incident is buried.
- Two years ago: Cade Mercer becomes the perfect son on paper, following his father Pastor Daniel Mercer's rules to protect his younger sister June from becoming the next target.
- One year ago: Ezra Quinn starts recording campus stories and quietly collecting old athletics-cover-up material connected to his brother's collapse.
- Present week, night one: A midnight donor run puts Ezra, Cade, and June in the same public frame. A flash drive labeled `Discipline Archive` changes hands by accident.
- Present season: A fake-dating deal grows into a real bond while the archive reveals donor, church, and athletics abuse.

## Character Profiles

### CHAR-01 Ezra Quinn

- Public Identity: campus audio-journalist, lit-mag editor, runner, and the boy donors dismiss as sharp but harmless
- Hidden Identity: younger brother of Noah Quinn and the only person stubborn enough to keep digging into the buried assault network
- Core Desire: expose the system without becoming emotionally owned by anyone inside it
- Core Fear: if he lets himself be loved, he will lose the one advantage he trusts, control
- Voice Style: dry, exact, unhurried, cutting when threatened
- Visual Design: dark wool coats, black training layers, silver headphones, pale notebook pages against cold blue light
- Wardrobe System: `LOOK-01` black runner-editor silhouette; `LOOK-06` donor-night black tailoring with metallic restraint
- Signature Props: `PROP-04`, `PROP-01`
- Relationship Notes: Ezra opens every scene from a position of verbal control. Cade is the first person who can force him off-script.

### CHAR-02 Cade Mercer

- Public Identity: St. Brendan's golden freshman winger, donor-family legacy athlete, poster face for clean discipline
- Hidden Identity: closeted abuse survivor whose life has been designed by fear, obedience, and damage control
- Core Desire: keep June safe long enough to become a person his father cannot own
- Core Fear: that every act of desire will cost somebody else their safety
- Voice Style: clipped, guarded, practical, then unexpectedly soft when he forgets to hide
- Visual Design: expensive athletic basics, navy practice layers, healing bruises, clean lines ruined by tension in the jaw and hands
- Wardrobe System: `LOOK-02` rink warmup prestige; `LOOK-03` donor-son formal armor
- Signature Props: `PROP-02`, `PROP-06`, `PROP-05`
- Relationship Notes: Cade begins as a pressure vessel, not a talker. His arc is learning to choose aloud instead of endure in silence.

### CHAR-03 Pastor Daniel Mercer

- Public Identity: church leader, donor broker, man of order, father of the college's most marketable freshman athlete
- Hidden Identity: a violent controller who confuses obedience with love and believes fear is moral leadership
- Core Desire: preserve family image and institutional leverage
- Core Fear: public exposure of private abuse
- Voice Style: gentle-commanding in public, surgically cruel in private
- Visual Design: charcoal overcoats, black leather gloves, expensive clergy-adjacent minimalism
- Wardrobe System: `LOOK-05` church-power blackcoat
- Signature Props: `PROP-02`, `PROP-06`
- Relationship Notes: Daniel must feel more dangerous when calm than when shouting.

### CHAR-04 June Mercer

- Public Identity: pastor's bright daughter, donor-event accessory, good church kid
- Hidden Identity: the only Mercer child actively smuggling proof out of the family house
- Core Desire: get out alive and make sure Cade does too
- Core Fear: being sent away, disbelieved, or returned home
- Voice Style: fast, bright, brave until panic breaks the rhythm
- Visual Design: school hoodie layers, oversized borrowed coats, bruised innocence
- Wardrobe System: `LOOK-04` escape hoodie and oversized winter coat
- Signature Props: `PROP-01`, `PROP-05`

- Visual Design: worn captain jacket, tape on wrists, practical gear

### Color And Material Logic

- Ezra world: black, charcoal, graphite, silver, paper white
- Cade world: navy, steel, dark green, bruise purple, clean athletic textures
- Mercer power world: black, oxblood, polished walnut, stained-glass jewel accents used coldly
- Safehouse / emotional thaw: amber, oatmeal knits, worn cotton, imperfect warmth

### Camera Personality

- Public spaces: harder lines, symmetrical pressure, witness-heavy frames
- Private spaces: closer lenses, soft practical light, breath and skin detail
- Hockey spaces: glass reflections, tunnel compression, ice glare, sound dampening before impact

## Continuity Rules

- Cade's bruises must progress logically after every major violence beat.
- Ezra always carries either `PROP-04` or visible recording equipment when in work mode.
- `PROP-01` and `PROP-03` must remain visually distinct from each other.
- Pastor Mercer never looks disordered in public, even after physical conflict.
- June's panic should appear in body rhythm before it appears in dialogue.
- Fake-dating public beats must always contrast with more honest private blocking.
- The rink must feel colder than every other recurring set.

## Narrative No-Go Zones

- Do not soften Daniel into a misunderstood strict parent.
- Do not let the school fix itself off-screen.
- Do not turn the romance into generic healing fluff; friction is part of the attraction.
- Do not let public proof arrive too early.
- Do not waste June as a passive hostage.

## AI Asset Generation Notes

- Ezra prompts should avoid soft-boy styling. He is elegant, severe, and intelligent before he is romantic.
- Cade prompts should avoid meathead-athlete clichés. He is beautiful because restraint is visibly failing.
- Pastor Mercer should read expensive, controlled, and frighteningly normal.
- St. Brendan interiors must feel funded, polished, and morally unsafe.
- The rink should combine prestige with exhaustion, never nostalgic coziness.
- Safehouse imagery should feel temporary and real, not luxury.
