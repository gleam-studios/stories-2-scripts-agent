- **Target Market**: NA-English

- **Protagonist Snapshot**: Nora weaponizes composure, evidence, and public witness.

---

## 2. Core Selling Points

- **Genre Hook**: Hidden-identity revenge romance set inside NA elite philanthropic society — every public event is a war stage.
- **Emotional Hook**: The pleasure of watching a dead woman walk back into the room that buried her and systematically dismantle everyone who profited from her disappearance.
- **Character Hook**: Nora never raises her voice. She wins by being the only person in the room who already knows the ending.
- **Differentiation**: Conflict lives entirely in public elite venues — galas, auctions, board rooms — where social witnesses are weaponized and every reversal is performed in front of cameras.

---

## 3. Adaptation Positioning

- **Core Hook**: The presumed-dead heiress walks into her family's gala under another name and starts dismantling their power in front of cameras.
- **Audience Positioning**: Female-skewing overseas short-drama audience seeking status reversal, revenge payoff, and high-tension romance.
- **Similarity Avoidance Direction**: Shift conflict into public elite venues, boardroom legitimacy warfare, and fast alliance pressure — not household confrontations.

---

## 4. World Setting Summary

- **Era & Environment**: Contemporary NA elite philanthropic society — old-money families with donor seats at universities, hospitals, and cultural boards. Power is performed publicly.
- **Social Rules**: Legitimacy is the currency. A name on a building buys authority; losing that name loses everything. Social humiliation in public spaces has real legal and economic consequences.
- **Industry / Special Setting**: The Hale family controls legitimacy through charitable foundations, board seats, and loyalist attorneys. Every social event is also a business meeting.
- **Key Constraints**:
  1. Nora cannot reveal her true identity until she has enough public proof to survive the legal counterattack.
  2. Evidence only has power if presented in a public setting where it cannot be quietly suppressed.
  3. Adrian operates under a fiduciary obligation that limits how openly he can assist Nora.

## 6. Main Plot

- **Protagonist Want**: Nora wants to reclaim her legal name and the Hale Foundation — publicly, irreversibly, and without surrendering the power she's built as Eva.
- **Antagonist Block**: Celia controls the board, attorneys, and social infrastructure; any private confrontation can be suppressed, so Nora must build public proof too visible to erase.
- **Escalation Path**:
  1. Nora enters as Eva Stone and begins collecting public proof — each appearance tightens the net around Celia's forged legitimacy.
  2. Celia identifies Eva as a genuine threat and escalates from social exclusion to institutional weaponization.
  3. A board director Nora trusted turns out to be part of the original erasure — forcing Nora to rebuild her alliance from scratch.
  4. The final confrontation requires Nora to make a public move that exposes herself before the proof is fully locked — a calculated risk she must consciously choose.
- **Resolution Direction**: Nora forces a public board vote with Miriam's testimony and the recovered documents — Celia's position collapses legally, and Nora reclaims the Hale name in the same room where she was first declared dead.

## 8. Overall Story Structure

### Opening
- **Protagonist Intro**: Nora enters the Hale charity gala as Eva Stone — composed, anonymous, and already planning.
- **Situation Setup**: The Hale Foundation thrives under Celia's public face; Nora's legal death has been filed, processed, and largely forgotten by everyone except the people who arranged it.
- **Inciting Event**: Celia publicly humiliates Eva without knowing who she is — and a camera catches the scar on Nora's wrist that only the real heiress would have.
- **First Hook**: Adrian notices Eva knows family-only information no outsider should have — and chooses not to say anything.

### Development
- **Protagonist Action**: Nora uses each public event as a surgical strike — surfacing one layer of forged legitimacy at a time, building public record without triggering a legal counterattack she isn't ready for.
- **Conflict Escalation**: Celia escalates from social exclusion to loyalist deployment to active board manipulation; Adrian's fiduciary position becomes increasingly untenable as evidence mounts.
- **Relationship Changes**: Nora and Adrian move from strategic mutual recognition to covert alliance to something that can no longer be called professional; Celia and Nora move from social warfare to open institutional combat.
- **Satisfaction / Crisis Pattern**: Each public event ends with Nora winning status and Celia losing ground — followed immediately by a private threat that erases the safety of the win. Rhythm: public high → private danger → harder move required.

### Midpoint Reversal
- **Partial Truth Reveal**: A board director Nora has been using as a passive ally is revealed to have signed the original erasure documents — he was not neutral; he was managed.
- **Major Setback**: The evidence chain Nora built over twelve episodes is legally discredited overnight by a document Celia filed six months before Nora returned.
- **Antagonist Gains**: Celia forces an emergency board vote that strips Eva of all public standing — and the room watches it happen.

### Climax
- **Ultimate Conflict**: Nora must decide whether to wait for safer proof or force the final public confrontation now, before Celia destroys the last remaining witness.
- **Biggest Secret Revealed**: The document erasing Nora's identity was signed by Nora's own father under legal duress — the forgery ring goes deeper than Celia, implicating foundation architects still on the board.
- **Relationship Reconstruction**: Adrian steps outside his fiduciary role publicly — a move that costs him his board seat and makes his position unambiguous.

### Ending
- **Main Plot Resolution**: Miriam testifies in an open board session; the recovered documents are read into the record. The board vote reverses. Celia's position collapses legally.
- **Character Fates**: Nora reclaims the Hale name and takes the foundation chair; Celia faces civil proceedings; Adrian resigns from the board by choice; Miriam's testimony enters the public archive.
- **Emotional Landing**: The final scene is Nora alone in the room where she was declared dead, signing her real name for the first time in years.
- **Season 2 Hook**: A letter arrives addressed to Nora Hale — postmarked three weeks before her father's death — suggesting the erasure was not Celia's idea originally.
