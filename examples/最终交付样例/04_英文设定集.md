## World Background & Positioning

**World Type:** Contemporary

**Power Structure:**  
The Hale world runs on performative legitimacy — public witness, donor networks, and corporate alliances. Whoever controls the room controls the narrative. Private truth is worthless until it lands in front of witnesses.

**Social Rules:**  
Status must be constantly performed and publicly defended. Backing down in public is permanent defeat. The person who stays composed under attack is assumed to be right.

**The Core Wound:**  
Nora was erased — her identity stolen, her death staged — so that wealth could be transferred before she could claim it. The system rewarded the theft.

**Tone Summary:**  
Cold luxury revenge: controlled devastation delivered in evening wear, in front of cameras.

## Visual System

**Overall Color Palette:**


| Slot      | Color                   |
| --------- | ----------------------- |
| Dominant  | Deep black (#0a0a0a)    |
| Accent    | Cold gold (#c9a84c)     |
| Shadow    | Navy void (#0d0d1f)     |
| Highlight | Brittle white (#f8f8f8) |


**Lighting Philosophy:**  
Harsh top light and cold fill — nothing is softened. Public spaces are over-lit like a courtroom. Private spaces use single-source cold task light. There is no warmth in this world except what characters perform.

**Texture Language:** satin · engraved metal · cold marble · pressed paper · cut crystal

**Hero vs. Villain Visual Contrast:**


|                 | Visual                                                                                                                    |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hero (Nora)     | Severe black silhouette, minimal surface, controlled negative space — reads as force through restraint                    |
| Villain (Celia) | Warm saturated palette, maximum surface decoration, high information density — reads as desperation dressed as confidence |


**Forbidden Aesthetics:**

- Warm filter or golden hour glow on any scene
- Pastel softness or romantic diffusion
- Handheld shaky-cam realism
- Naturalistic clutter in Nora's spaces
- Any shot that makes Celia look vulnerable or sympathetic before Episode 30

## Narrative Forbidden Zones

1. Nora must never beg, cry first, or physically retreat from a confrontation — she may be wounded, but she does not break in front of an enemy.
2. The family seal must never appear damaged, incomplete, or visually different from its established description.
3. Celia must not be portrayed sympathetically or as a victim before Episode 30 — her suffering is a consequence of her own choices, not audience manipulation material.
4. Adrian must not be definitively established as innocent or guilty before Episode 25 — his moral ambiguity is a core dramatic engine.
5. No character may reveal they know Eva is Nora before the designed reveal window without an on-screen mechanism that seals their silence.
