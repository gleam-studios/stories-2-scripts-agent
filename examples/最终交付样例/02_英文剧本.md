## EPISODE 01

[EPISODE BRIEF]
Episode Plot: Nora enters the gala as Eva Stone. Celia strikes first in public; Nora deflects without breaking composure. The fight moves to a private corridor where family portraits watch. Adrian interrupts before Celia can land a killing blow — and all three understand the war has started.
Opening Hook: A stranger walks into a room that buried her — and the woman on the throne doesn't know it yet.
Cold Open Focus: Ballroom in motion, crystal light, Nora alone at the entrance with a champagne glass she does not drink.
Closing Hook: Adrian asks a question only someone inside the family war would dare answer — Nora answers without flinching; he still doesn't look away.
Core Satisfaction: Status reversal — Celia's public dismissal fails clean; Nora takes the social high ground in one line.
Target Emotion: Controlled exhilaration — the audience knows Nora is dangerous before anyone in the room does.

### SCENE 01.01
Time: Night
Location: INT. HALE CHARITY GALA
Characters: NORA HALE / EVA STONE, CELIA HALE, ADRIAN VALE
Core Event: Celia publicly attempts to socially eject Eva at the entrance; Eva deflects and takes the high ground
Scene Result: Celia is left without a clean move; Adrian, watching from across the room, clocks that Eva knows something she shouldn't

[OPENING STATE]
The ballroom is in full donor-gala motion — guests in careful formation, conversations managed at a distance. Crystal overhead. Warm gold light. But the room has an edge beneath the polish.

Nora steps through the entrance alone. She doesn't scan for exits — she already knows them. A champagne glass lifted from the nearest tray. She holds it without drinking.

Across the room, CELIA HALE receives a guest. Her smile is the practiced kind.

[MAIN BODY]
Celia clocks Eva. Her smile doesn't break — but something behind it sharpens. She crosses the room with the ease of a woman who owns it.

She stops directly in Eva's path.

CELIA
You must be lost.

Nora turns to her. Unhurried. Holds the look one beat too long before answering.

NORA
Not at all. I came exactly where I was invited.

Celia steps closer, lowering her voice under the ambient noise.

CELIA
I don't know who sent you through those doors. But this is a private event.

Nora glances at the room — the donors, the circling photographers, the recording crew — then back at Celia. All of it registered. None of it rushed.

NORA
Then you should have locked them better.

A beat. Celia's smile has tightened one degree. She has nothing clean left to say.

Adrian Vale stands at the edge of the frame, wine glass loose in hand. He has been watching since Nora walked in. His expression gives nothing. But he hasn't moved on.

[SCENE ENDING]
A hotel photographer's camera sweeps across the entrance group for the donor recap reel — and for just a moment, the frame catches Nora's wrist. The scar. Small. Old. Just enough.

### SCENE 01.02
Time: Night
Location: INT. GALA SIDE CORRIDOR
Characters: NORA HALE / EVA STONE, CELIA HALE, ADRIAN VALE
Core Event: Celia steers Eva into the private corridor for a harder confrontation; Adrian interrupts before Celia can land the final move
Scene Result: All three know the social war has started; the portrait nameplate is caught in frame for one beat too long

[OPENING STATE]
The corridor runs behind the main ballroom — narrower, quieter, the ambient music now muffled. Both walls are lined with Hale family portraits. Old money on canvas, stretching back three generations.

The last portrait on the left: a nameplate reads NORA HALE — HEIRESS. Tarnished brass. It has not been updated.

[MAIN BODY]
Celia steers Eva out of the ballroom with social pressure alone — a hand, a redirect, a closed smile that isn't an invitation.

In the corridor, Celia turns. The narrower space is deliberate.

CELIA
Whatever trick sent you through those doors ends tonight.

Nora doesn't step back. She glances, once, at the portrait directly above Celia's shoulder — the real Nora Hale, oil on canvas — and then back at Celia.

NORA
Then you should have locked them better.

Celia holds the silence. Her jaw tightens almost imperceptibly.

Adrian's footstep on marble, offscreen. He rounds the far end of the corridor, wine glass still in hand.

ADRIAN
If you're done threatening the guests — I need Miss Stone for the Patterson account. He's waiting.

He looks between them. Unhurried.

CELIA
We were finishing a private conversation.

ADRIAN
It looked finished from where I was standing.

[SCENE ENDING]
Celia holds Adrian's gaze for a moment, then walks away without answering. Adrian watches her go. Then he turns to Nora.

His expression is not concern. It is something closer to assessment.

ADRIAN
(quiet)
You know the Patterson family?

Nora meets his eyes.

NORA
I know a great deal about this foundation.

Adrian says nothing. But he doesn't look away.

## EPISODE 02

[EPISODE BRIEF]
Episode Plot: Nora tracks down Miriam Cross — the retired archivist who holds independent verification of the Hale identity trail. Their first meeting is cautious, coded, and dangerous for both. By the end, Miriam opens her portfolio without handing anything over — and names something only an insider would recognize.
Opening Hook: The one witness everyone needs is the one person who chose silence for years.
Cold Open Focus: Institutional waiting room, muffled board session behind a closed door, Miriam alone with a leather portfolio on her lap.
Closing Hook: Miriam says Nora has her mother's way of standing — and Nora does not deny it; the folder stays open between them.
Core Satisfaction: Truth drop tease — information is offered as presence, not as a gift yet.
Target Emotion: Tight hope under fear — the audience feels the key is in the room but not yet turned.

### SCENE 02.01
Time: Day
Location: INT. HALE FOUNDATION BOARDROOM - ANTECHAMBER
Characters: NORA HALE / EVA STONE, MIRIAM CROSS
Core Event: Nora finds Miriam Cross waiting outside the board session and makes first contact
Scene Result: Miriam recognizes something she wasn't expecting and chooses to stay — cautiously

[OPENING STATE]
A waiting area outside the main boardroom. Institutional light, chairs no one uses comfortably. A folder of meeting materials sits closed on the side table. From inside, the muffled sound of a board session in progress.

MIRIAM CROSS sits alone — late sixties, posture precise, a single leather portfolio on her lap. She does not look like someone who was invited. She looks like someone who came anyway.

[MAIN BODY]
Nora enters from the side hall, catching sight of Miriam before Miriam registers her.

Nora stops. Takes her in. Something shifts in her expression — controlled, but present.

She approaches and takes the chair beside Miriam instead of the one across.

NORA
Miriam Cross.

Miriam looks up. Her eyes settle on Nora's face for a moment longer than a stranger's would.

MIRIAM
I'm sorry. Do we know each other?

NORA
Not yet.

Miriam's grip on the portfolio tightens — just slightly.

NORA (CONT'D)
You were the Hale Foundation's chief archivist for nineteen years. You retired the same month the estate settlement was finalized.

A beat. Miriam says nothing.

NORA (CONT'D)
I've been looking for you.

MIRIAM
People look for me when they want things I can't give them.

NORA
I'm looking for things you already have.

Miriam studies her. The muffled board session continues behind the closed door.

[SCENE ENDING]
Miriam opens her portfolio. Inside: a stack of documents, ordered carefully. She does not hand them over. She does not close the folder.

MIRIAM
You have your mother's way of standing.

Nora doesn't answer. She didn't come here to confirm anything — not yet.
